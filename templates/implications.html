Future Vision

From Demonstration to Methodology

This dashboard demonstrates specification sensitivity in one pipeline. The goal is to extend this approach into a formal tool that can be used in every pipeline, every domain, every high-stakes decision system.

The Vision: SpecCheck as a Standard

From AtlasED to Universal Practice

SpecCheck becomes an open-source library with three standardized metrics, applicable to any analytical pipeline, running as a pre-deployment check, a CI job, or a runtime logging layer.

For Engineers: "Run SpecCheck before deployment" becomes as routine as "run tests." The library outputs a specification sensitivity report — a PDF or dashboard showing how findings vary across perturbations.

For Institutions: Every deployed model includes a SpecCheck disclosure — a public statement of the specification choices made and the sensitivity of findings to those choices.

For Affected Users: The basis to ask: "Would the decision have been different under a different specification?" This is contestability at the infrastructure level.

Extending to Other Domains

Mental Health

Climate Change

Criminal Justice
Risk assessment tools where the choice of features and training data shapes life-changing decisions.
Financial Services
Credit scoring models where specification sensitivity could reveal discriminatory patterns.

Why This Benefits Everyone

For Researchers Specification sensitivity is not only a quality check. It is a discovery method. Every perturbation shown in this dashboard produced findings about the education system that were inaccessible from any single model run.
For Institutions Auditable evidence that specification choices were tested. When a decision is contested, the institution can show: "We tested this under five different specifications. Here's what changed and what stayed robust."
For Those Affected by Decisions The basis to ask whether the decision would have been different under a different specification. This is the foundation of meaningful AI contestability — not just "explain the output," but "show me what would change if the choices were different."

Next Steps

Short-term: Develop the SpecCheck library with three core metrics: specification reproducibility, finding stability, and disclosure completeness.
