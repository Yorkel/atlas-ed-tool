What This Means

Core Finding The same question produces different answers depending on which documents the AI uses. With media included, the focus is on crises — funding, Ofsted, strikes. Without it, the focus shifts to structural issues — SEND, system fragmentation, post-16 barriers. Both are valid, but neither is complete. Removing media changed 93% of topics the model found — same model, same settings. One source, Schools Week, accounts for roughly 70% of the dataset, and nine of thirty topics are more than 90% from a single source. At that point, the model is discovering who publishes most — not what the policy landscape actually contains.
Core Insight Media connects different parts of the system but also frames issues as crises. Without it, perspectives fragment but reveal deeper structural problems — regulatory frameworks, research agendas, and consultation processes that were invisible before. The question isn't which version is right, but which one is shaping decisions — and whether decision-makers know.
