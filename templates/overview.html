What Can AI Tell Us About Education Policy-and What Shapes Its Answers?

AI is shaping real decisions in education systems - including the issues that get attention. But the choices shaping AI outputs aren't always visible. Builders can't easily show them, decision makers can't assess them, and those affected can't question them.

AtlasED analyses thousands of education policy documents to surface what's being debated, who's shaping the conversation, and how these patterns change over time. But it also does something most AI tools don't: it shows you what changes when the choices behind the model change.

Why This Matters A policymaker asks AI what the biggest issue in education is. The model says 'learning outcomes,' so funding follows. Change one model setting, and that single category splits into reading scores, maths attainment, progress rates, and early years development - each needing different action. The result wasn't wrong. It depended on how the question was set up.

The Core Model

The model used here is a straightforward machine learning method that reads documents, groups them, and identifies topics. We chose it deliberately - because every choice is visible. The number of topics, the data sources, the text processing - change any one and watch the findings shift. Here you can see what the core model found - and what it would find if different choices were made.
